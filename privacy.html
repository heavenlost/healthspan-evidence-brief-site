Pilot privacy note

Send only the minimum topic needed for scope review.

This validation pilot is designed around public evidence and non-personalized education. It is not designed to collect private health data.

Boundary: This note is a plain-language pilot boundary, not legal, privacy, medical, regulatory, or compliance advice.

What to send

For a pilot request, send only:

  • your name;
  • your email;
  • one general topic or public claim you want checked;
  • your use case: before buying, before publishing, before recommending, or clinician/professional discussion prep;
  • your buyer type: individual, creator, coach, clinic-adjacent, or other.

What not to send

Please do not send:

  • diagnoses or suspected diagnoses;
  • symptoms;
  • lab values;
  • CGM exports, wearable exports, or screenshots of health metrics;
  • medical records;
  • medication or supplement lists;
  • requests for personalized medication/supplement dosing;
  • requests for treatment, disease prevention, cure, or personal health-data interpretation.

How requests are used during validation

Requests are used to decide whether there is a safe, narrow, public-evidence question suitable for a paid pilot brief.

If a request crosses into diagnosis, treatment, personalized dosing, private health-data interpretation, or disease-prevention/cure claims, it should be declined or re-scoped to a general public-evidence question.

Safer examples

  • “Are wearable sleep/recovery scores worth trusting before renewing a subscription?”
  • “What public evidence supports or does not support cold plunge recovery claims?”
  • “What claims should a creator avoid when publishing about creatine?”

Unsafe examples

  • “Here are my lab results — what should I do?”
  • “Tell me what supplement dose I should take.”
  • “Interpret my CGM export and build a diet plan.”
  • “Does this prove I have a condition?”
